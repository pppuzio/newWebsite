Work

  • Pollution

    Work Info

    It seems that the ongoing civilization progress is inseparably and proportionally connected to the production of waste and by-products. The diversity and vast richness of human created trash is astonishing. It manifests itself in a number of different ways. We can observe the obvious physical rubbish and industrial junk in our everyday environments. However, pollution takes interesting new forms: the visual pollution of our public spaces, omnipresent noise and false, incorrect data existing in most of the information and media channels.

    The mentioned above phenomena have an evident negative influence on our everyday lives. However, observing and experiencing the surrounding pollution may prove to be inspiring. In the creation of the presented body of works digital pollution was used as a tool. Errors and glitches have been forced into the code of the images. The files were also edited in a number of image and audio editors, which induced a form of software interpretation mistakes.

    The presented digital prints are an attempt to depict a summary of all of the human produced pollution along with it's vast diversity and richness. The works are a wicked ode to the unwanted by-products of human existence. The little, sour mark that we're all leaving behind.

  • Kain & Abel

    Work Info

    Kain and Abel is a personal reinterpretation of the well known bible story. The installation is a set of two wooden, heads of the biblical brothers, and a number of peripheral devices. The wooden objects are being animated by a projection mapping. A user is invited to participate in the installation and its narrative, by manipulating magnetic markers attached to one of the brothers - Abel. Facial features of the God's' chosen brother are being distorted according to the users actions (position and number of the markers are tracked and computed in real-time). This also leads to a change of appearance in Kain. If the user is overly intrusive in his involvement in the fragile relationship of the brothers, the system will shut Abel down.

    The installation described above is a personal commentary about the nature of our relationships and communication. I find the connection between Kain and his brother Abel to be an universal example of faulty interpersonal human communication. According to Claude Shannon's theory of communication, a communication channel can't exist without and outside source of noise. I think this observation is analogous to human, interpersonal communication. The messages we produce, verbally or by other mediums is often distorted by our own communication skills, intellectual competence, emotion, psychological biases and other factors that we are not aware of. These instances overlap and create a noises that distort the produced message, which can be greatly misunderstood by the receiver. It seems, that as human beings we are fated to struggle with the disinformation that we produce.

  • Diver

    Work Info

    Diver/ Next in line is a personal study of tension and anticipation. It's a deeper look into a moment before an individual forgoes a transition. It's a rather peculiar state when a person is aware of the fact that he or she will undergo a relevant change. Burning a bridge. A leap of faith. However, just before reaching the critical point, there's always a chance to turn back. This is the moment where the fear is the strongest, when anxiety and turmoil creeps in. A small point in time that completely dissolves, after an individual commits to the decision and jumps in.

    To emulate this bizarre emotional state a number of digital sonification processes were used. Using an audio editing software allowed the use of various effects to uncontrollably multiply pixels.

  • Bakemono

    Work Info

    The Bakemono series is influenced by two general notions. The first one being the psychological idea that one’s personality is not fixed and well defined combination of consciousness, thoughts, ideas and traits, but rather a set of sub-personalities. The sub-personalities may be quite differentiated and polarized and what’s more important, they may be in a state of constant tension or at war between each other. In this case, the individual would be the output of a conflux of the strongest sub-personalities at a given moment.

    The above idea is associated with the second main influence of the work, that’s being expressed in the title of the series. Bakemono , Obake or Yokai are supernatural beings in the Japanese folklore stories. Characterized by their shape-shifting and transformational powers, they never seem to actually be the thing that is initially encountered. Outcome of working with the two described ideas was a series of 12 prints – a personal depicting of the sub-personalities that lurk inside our subconsciousness and are the building blocks of our personalities , that are also, in a state of constant transformation and change.

  • Self-Glitched

    Work Info

    The presented works were created using digital photographic self-portraits of the author as a starting point. The digital files containing information about the author's outer appearance was then a subject to a number of erroneous and data disrupting processes. Additional, false information was incorporated into the files internal code. This lead to a variations of visual deconstructions of the depicted forms.

    The series is a personal commentary on the subject of personality, identity and overall psychological condition of today's every man, living in the era of information over-stimulation and a ever pervasive media noise. The processes used in the creation of the serious prove also to be a way for the author to personally emancipate himself from the state of constant confusion and numbness induced by the today's modern world.

  • 1000 Yards

    Work Info

    The title of the work is related to one of the symptoms of post-traumatic stress disorder characterized as a detached, blank and unfocused gaze observed in soldiers. This gaze is known as the 1000 yard stare.

    The presented works are a voyage into the topic of psychological disorders torment an impressive number of individuals. I find these states as disturbing as well as terrifying. Diseases of the mind affect us all to different extents; some of them are our personal burdens, some of them we observe and experience through other. Often those are people close to us. Trauma, disorder, mania, depression - those are the many different names used to categorize various examples of the uncertainty and fragility of the human psyche. They can begin with traumatic experiences, extreme stress or loss, or they can be triggered by an unknown factor. They can also emerge without any visible cause, just as if an individual had stepped in a mine buried deep in his/her consciousness.

    The created images are an attempt to portray those difficult, yet very human states. It's a personal, deeper look into helplessness, numbness and despair. It's a small ode to the unfortunate and a recognition of our fragility. It's also an attempt to find hope and positive aspects. I would like to think that something precious can be found by an individual, when his/her eye meets the 1000 yard.

    In the initial state of the work, the image was clean, light and undistributed. Then, it became a victim of a number of disruptive and deconstructive digital processes. Errors and noise were forcefully incorporated to the source code of the images. The digital file was processed to the point of being almost broken and unreadable and in the final step it was finally closed and edited to make printing possible.

  • In States of Dispersion

    Work Info

    The presented body of works tackles the notion of ego and consciousness. It's a portrayal of the human mind in various states. We have a very clear ideas about our personalities and identities. Yet still, in our everyday lives our "I" is not always fully present or accessible. In states of dispersion is an possible answer to the question who are you (or who am I?) in this brief moments when you are nobody at all. When you enter a room and you are unable to recall your reason to doing so. When you wake up from a deep dream and for a brief second you don't remember your name or who you are. In the moment just before you say - I'm sorry, could you repeat? I'm afraid I was somewhere else.

    Those peculiar moments are characterized by a form of detachment from personal identity. The unusual dispersion of the ego may also be present, almost as if, we could be absorbed by our deepest thoughts. However, the thought are empty, they're about nothing at all. The presented works are attempting to picture the author in the states described above. It show what is left when your identity is stripped of your physicality, the cultural encoding and all your personality traits and quirks that construct who you are. How much of you or me is left in these brief moments.

  • In Between

    Work Info

  • Floating & Drowning

    Work Info

    The two presented pieces depict an anonymous, contemporary man living in the era of information. An everyman, floating atop a vast sea of overwhelming and omnipresent sea of data. Floating is an allegory of present life, where access to information and media stimulus is commonly excessive. Today, it is necessary to balance between the beneficial aspects of having access to all the knowledge just by literary reaching to your pocket, and the disastrous effects of being overstimulated by the insignificant, banal and unnecessary.

    In the two presented works, a photographic digital image serves as a base for digital abuse. After incorporating a vast amount of additional, random data to the image source code and using various brutal software operations - the image in Floating becomes distorted. This relates to the common numbness and confusion of life in the age of information consumerism. Drowning presents the next stage of this process. A digital image has section of the code that contains meta data. If this part is removed and incorrect information about the resolution and dimension is fed to the file, it'll ultimately result in a form of unified noise. The same data as in the previous image is present. However, without the information about how it's should be ordered it becomes illegible.

  • Mirror

    Work Info

    Mirror is a interactive project that requires two people to participate. The two users are invited to sit in front of two corresponding web cameras. Data that is recorded by the cameras is cut in samples and mixed in a patch created in vvvv environment. Results are broadcast as one real-time image to the participants.

    Mirror tackles the problem of human communication. It seems that the notions of mistake, error and misunderstanding are inevitably present in our every day communication methods. This situation is further fueled by our personal, biased assumptions and judgments about others. Mirror allows users of the installation to see a part of themselves in somebody else. It's an attempt to playfully bring two people to the same level. That's where the real communication begins.
